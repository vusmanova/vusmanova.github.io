Bukhara

Bukhara is one of the oldest cities in Central Asia and one of the greatest shopping centers on the Silk Road. Also, Bukhara is a museum city where you can see more than 140 architectural monuments of the Middle Ages. This historic city in inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. Attactions such as the small Samanid mausoleum, which recently celebrated its millenium, Kosh Madras, the Kalon Minaret and the Poi Kalyan architectural complex, were built about 2300 years ago and attract many tourists.

Samanid Mausoleum

Picture by David Stanley [CC BY 2.0], via Wikimedia Commons

The Samanid Mausoleum (aka Ismail Samani Mausoleum) was built during the life of this ruler, one of the most prominent representatives of the dynasty, who ruled Bukhara from 892 to 907. Initially, the mausoleum was intended for the tomb of Ismail's father, Ahmad, but later became a tomb for all members of the royal family. This is oldest Muslim landmark in Bukhara and a real masterpiece of early Islamic architecture.

Minaret Kalyan

Another architectural symbol of Bukhara is the Kalyan Minaret. It was built at the beginning of the XII century and is a tall tower of burnt bricks 47 meters high. Its foundation goes down 10 meters. The main purpose of minaret is to call the Bukhara people to prayer. But its impressive height began to serve many other purposes. For example, it was a lighthouse in the sandy desert sea for lost caravans. A beautiful panorama of the area opened from here, which made it possible to calculate the enemy long before his approaches to the city.
